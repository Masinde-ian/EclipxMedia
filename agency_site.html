Discovery & Business Alignment

Before any channel strategy is discussed, we conduct a structured discovery sprint that maps your actual business model — not just your marketing goals. This means understanding unit economics: what is your CAC threshold before the business breaks even? What is the LTV:CAC ratio required to justify paid acquisition at scale? What does your sales cycle look like, and where does it create misalignment with last-click attribution?

Most agencies skip this step and build campaigns against the wrong success metric. We've seen accounts optimising for CPL on a product with a 9-month sales cycle — a structural mismatch that no amount of bidding strategy adjustment will fix. Alignment on business outcomes precedes all tactical execution.

// 03 — Performance & Optimization Audits

The audit is the
foundation, not the sales pitch.

We issue a structured audit before every engagement. What we find determines the scope of work — not a pre-written proposal.
Audit Philosophy
Duration: 5–7 business days
Access required: Ad accounts, GA4, GSC, CRM
Output: 40–80 page audit report
Cost: Complimentary for qualified accounts

Every audit we conduct begins with the assumption that the data we're being shown is partially wrong. Not because clients are dishonest, but because the tools that generate that data are structurally imperfect: browser-based pixels that fire inconsistently, attribution windows that over-credit last-click, and GA4 configurations that have never been validated against actual conversion volume.

Our audit framework is divided into four layers: measurement integrity, account structure, creative performance, and competitive positioning. Each layer has a defined scoring rubric, and critical failures in measurement integrity automatically trigger a tracking rebuild recommendation before any media strategy changes are proposed.

AUDIT LAYER 01 //

Ad Account Structural Audit

We examine campaign architecture against platform best practices and your specific business model. This includes campaign-to-ad-set ratios, budget allocation logic, bidding strategy alignment with conversion window, audience overlap at ad-set level, and exclusion logic integrity. A structurally broken account wastes 20–40% of spend before any optimisation takes effect.

Campaign Architecture Audience Overlap Budget Allocation Bidding Strategy Exclusion Logic
AUDIT LAYER 02 //

Creative Fatigue Analysis

We pull historical creative performance data and model frequency curves against CTR decay rates. This produces a creative lifecycle map: which ads are in growth phase, plateau, or active decline. We identify the rotation failure points — the point at which fatigued creative is dragging overall account CPM up due to quality score degradation — and build a refresh calendar based on actual spend velocity and audience size.

Frequency Analysis CTR Decay Modelling Hook Rate Benchmarking Creative Rotation Schedule
AUDIT LAYER 03 //

Pixel & Tracking Implementation Audit

We map every conversion event against its implementation method: browser-based pixel, server-side CAPI, or enhanced conversions. We verify event deduplication logic, test for double-firing, check parameter passing accuracy (value, currency, event_id), and cross-reference total reported conversions against CRM closed-won data for the preceding 90 days. Any discrepancy above 15% triggers a rebuild recommendation as Priority 1.

Event Deduplication CAPI Signal Match Rate GA4 Schema Validation CRM Reconciliation Parameter Accuracy
AUDIT LAYER 04 //

Competitive Intelligence Mapping

Using Meta Ad Library, SpyFu, SemRush Share of Voice, and LinkedIn Ads Transparency data, we construct a competitive landscape map across paid and organic channels. We identify which competitors are scaling spend (a leading indicator of profitable unit economics), which keywords are under-contested in your category, and where share-of-voice gaps represent immediate expansion opportunities with verifiable demand.

Technical Challenge

Stratum's Google Search account contained 340 active keywords, the majority on broad match, with no negative keyword list maintained in over 14 months. Search term reports showed 62% of impressions serving against non-commercial queries. Simultaneously, LinkedIn retargeting targeted all website visitors with a single ad format — no distinction between a pricing page visitor and a blog reader. The conversion tracking stack relied exclusively on a browser-based LinkedIn Insight Tag with no server-side backup; in an audience of predominantly corporate IT environments with aggressive ad-blockers, signal loss was estimated at 38%. MQL scoring was based on form fill completion only, with no behavioural layer, meaning sales received leads from search terms with zero historical close rate.

Google Ads — Broad Match AuditLinkedIn Insight TagHubSpot CRMNo Negative Keyword ManagementBrowser-Side Only Tracking58% Attribution Discrepancy
Strategy Deployed

Phase 1: Full keyword architecture rebuild. Collapsed 340 keywords to 94 tightly grouped exact and phrase-match terms across 4 intent tiers. Deployed a 600-term negative keyword list built from 18 months of search term data. Bid strategies shifted to Target CPA per intent tier with separate conversion actions mapped to pipeline stage.

Phase 2: LinkedIn retargeting segmented into 5 audience pools by page depth and session time. Conversation Ads introduced for decision-maker titles against a matched audience built from CRM closed-won firmographic data.

Phase 3: Server-side LinkedIn CAPI implementation via sGTM, reducing signal loss from 38% to estimated 8%. HubSpot lead scoring rebuilt with behavioural data including page sequence, pricing page visits, return session count, and asset downloads. Sales received only leads meeting a composite score threshold, reducing SDR noise by 44%.

Technical Challenge

The Meta account contained 14 active campaigns with no coherent segmentation — prospecting and retargeting audiences overlapped across 9 of 14 campaigns, meaning the platform competed with itself in the auction and artificially inflated CPMs. The pixel was browser-based only, with no server-side CAPI, resulting in estimated 28–34% event loss post-iOS 14.5. Purchase events fired inconsistently: audit revealed duplicate purchase fires on ~19% of transactions, overstating reported ROAS by an estimated 0.6x. The top-spending ad had run for 11 months without a controlled variant. Frequency: 8.4 in the 30-day window. TikTok generated zero attributable conversions against $4,200 monthly spend.

14 Overlapping CampaignsNo Server-Side CAPI19% Duplicate Purchase FiresFrequency 8.428–34% Event Loss
Strategy Deployed

Week 1: Deployed Meta CAPI via sGTM with event deduplication logic using a composite event_id. Purchase event parity confirmed at 94% signal match. Duplicate pixel fires eliminated via GTM trigger sequencing.

Weeks 2–4: Collapsed 14 campaigns to 3: Cold Prospecting (Advantage+ creative), Warm Retargeting (1–7 day site visitors segmented by product page depth), and Retention (purchasers 1–90 days, cross-sell sequences). Audience exclusion logic enforced — zero overlap verified.

Weeks 4–8: 3×3 creative test matrix deployed: 3 hook variants × 3 value proposition framings. Each ad ran at $200/day with success criteria defined in advance (CTR >2.8%, ROAS >3.0x at 500 impression minimum). TikTok rebuilt with Spark Ads using UGC from top 12% of customers by LTV.

What Happens in Phase 01

The first two weeks of every engagement are exclusively diagnostic. We do not adjust bids, restructure campaigns, or launch creative until we have a complete, verified picture of the existing account state. Days 1–3: access and instrumentation — we receive read-only access to all ad platforms, GA4, CRM, and existing reporting infrastructure, then verify that the data we're seeing is accurate. Days 4–10: the audit itself, structured across four measurement layers. Days 11–14: synthesis and presentation — a 40–80 page Audit Report is produced and walked through in a 90-minute structured session with your team, during which findings are prioritised collectively and the 90-day remediation roadmap is agreed.

PHASE 02 //

Creative Sprints & Asset Generation

Week 3

Creative is built before campaigns launch. We do not launch with placeholder assets and iterate reactively. Every creative unit enters the account with a defined hypothesis and a pre-specified success metric.

Creative Framework Architecture

Week 3 is an intensive creative sprint producing the initial asset matrix for Phase 03 campaigns. Every creative element is built against a defined hypothesis — a prediction about which message, format, or visual approach will outperform alternatives for a specific audience and funnel stage. This means creative is never subjective internally. We can tell you exactly why we chose a particular hook, why we structured a value proposition in a specific order, and what we expect to learn from each variant relative to the others.

The sprint produces three creative tiers: Challenger concepts (high-risk, high-potential — format or message innovations with no precedent in the account), Control concepts (validated direct-response frameworks adapted to your brand positioning), and Evergreen concepts (brand-safe, long-lifetime assets for retargeting and retention audiences). Compliance-dependent verticals receive a fourth tier: Approved Control — pre-cleared templates with variables constrained to compliant ranges, enabling testing without re-triggering review cycles.

Ad Copy Framework — Structural Components
CF.01Hook layer (0–3 seconds / first line): Three variants per ad — Pain Hook (lead with the problem the audience already has), Outcome Hook (lead with the result they want), and Curiosity Hook (lead with a pattern interrupt or counterintuitive claim). Hook selection mapped to audience temperature: cold audiences respond more strongly to Outcome; warm retargeting audiences to Pain.
CF.02Value proposition body: Feature-benefit-proof structure. Each benefit claim is supported by a specific, named data point or client outcome from verified case study output. No generic superlatives ("industry-leading", "world-class"). No claims that cannot be sourced from CRM win data.
CF.03CTA architecture: Action-specific CTAs mapped to funnel stage — awareness ("See How It Works"), consideration ("Get the Benchmark Report"), decision ("Book a 20-Minute Audit"). CTAs are tested as controlled variables in isolation from hook and body copy to produce clean data on their independent contribution to conversion rate.
CF.04Visual asset matrix: 3 format types per concept (static image, carousel, short-form video) × 2 aspect ratios (1:1, 9:16). Mobile-first composition on all assets. Text overlay density checked against platform readability benchmarks. Contrast ratios verified for accessibility compliance.
PHASE 03 //

Deployment & Alpha Testing

Week 4

Controlled budget deployment with single-variable test isolation. We learn before we scale. Alpha phase exit requires verified creative controls, confirmed audience economics, and tracking parity below 10% discrepancy.

Controlled Deployment Protocol

Week 4 is the first live spend week. All campaigns launch at a defined Alpha Budget — typically 20–30% of total monthly budget — to generate learning data before committing full budget to unvalidated hypotheses. Alpha campaigns run in strict variable isolation: one creative variable tested per campaign, with audience, bid strategy, and placement held constant across all variants. Any performance differential is attributable to the tested variable, not confounded by simultaneous changes.

Audience architecture deploys across three temperature tiers from Day 1: Cold Prospecting, Warm Retargeting, and Retention. These run independently with audience exclusions enforced to prevent auction overlap. Alpha budget allocation is 60/30/10 (Prospecting/Retargeting/Retention) — deliberately overweighted toward prospecting to accelerate learning on net-new audiences, where unit economics are most uncertain and most consequential to scale decisions.

Alpha Testing Decision Framework
AT.01Minimum data threshold: No optimisation decision is made before 500 impressions per individual ad variant. No budget reallocation before statistical significance is met (p<0.05 using our Bayesian test model, which weights decisions by revenue-per-variant rather than conversion rate alone).
AT.02Weekly kill/scale criteria: Ads underperforming control by >25% on primary KPI at 1,000 impressions are paused and documented. Ads outperforming by >20% receive incremental budget increase of 20% per iteration to validate performance at scale before full budget commitment.
AT.03Audience test sequencing: New audience segments introduced one per week during Alpha phase. No more than two audience variables tested simultaneously in any single campaign. Overlap verification run weekly using platform Audience Overlap tool with documented results.
AT.04Alpha exit criteria: Phase 03 concludes when at least one creative control exists per funnel stage, audience segment economics are verified against target thresholds, and tracking parity is confirmed at <10% CRM discrepancy across all active conversion events.
PHASE 04 //

Scaling & Matrix Optimisation

Ongoing — Monthly Sprints

Scale is earned by verified economics, applied sequentially, and monitored continuously. We do not apply spend increases to unvalidated hypotheses regardless of external pressure to accelerate growth.

Automated Scaling Rules & Manual Override Protocol

Ongoing management operates on a rule-based scaling architecture with weekly manual review gates. Automated rules handle micro-optimisations within defined parameters — bid adjustments within ±15% of target, budget pacing corrections within daily windows, ad scheduling adjustments based on verified hourly conversion data — freeing strategist attention for macro decisions requiring contextual judgment that automated systems cannot replicate.

Manual override is required for any change exceeding 20% budget adjustment, any new audience introduction, any campaign structure modification, or any creative rotation above the planned cadence. This prevents automation from compounding errors without human review. The weekly sprint Loom exists specifically to assess whether automated rules are behaving as intended and to make manual corrections before they have downstream consequences. Every automated rule is documented in Notion with its trigger logic, expected behaviour, and override conditions before it is activated.
