Technical Challenge

Shamba OS's Google Search account contained 340 active keywords, the majority on broad match, with no negative keyword list maintained in over 14 months. Search term reports showed 62% of impressions serving against non-commercial queries. Simultaneously, LinkedIn retargeting targeted all website visitors with a single ad format — no distinction between a pricing page visitor and a blog reader. The conversion tracking stack relied exclusively on a browser-based LinkedIn Insight Tag with no server-side backup; in an audience of predominantly corporate IT environments with aggressive ad-blockers, signal loss was estimated at 38%. MQL scoring was based on form fill completion only, with no behavioural layer, meaning sales received leads from search terms with zero historical close rate.

Google Ads — Broad Match AuditLinkedIn Insight TagHubSpot CRMNo Negative Keyword ManagementBrowser-Side Only Tracking58% Attribution Discrepancy
Strategy Deployed

Phase 1: Full keyword architecture rebuild. Collapsed 340 keywords to 94 tightly grouped exact and phrase-match terms across 4 intent tiers. Deployed a 600-term negative keyword list built from 18 months of search term data. Bid strategies shifted to Target CPA per intent tier with separate conversion actions mapped to pipeline stage.

Phase 2: LinkedIn retargeting segmented into 5 audience pools by page depth and session time. Conversation Ads introduced for decision-maker titles against a matched audience built from CRM closed-won firmographic data.

Phase 3: Server-side LinkedIn CAPI implementation via sGTM, reducing signal loss from 38% to estimated 8%. HubSpot lead scoring rebuilt with behavioural data including page sequence, pricing page visits, return session count, and asset downloads. Sales received only leads meeting a composite score threshold, reducing SDR noise by 44%.

Final Quantifiable Outcome

At the 6-month mark, cost-per-SQL declined from KES 12,000 to KES 3,800 — a 68% reduction verified against CRM closed-won records. Monthly SQL volume increased from 8 to 19 despite total ad spend remaining flat at KES 50,000/month. MQL-to-SQL rate improved from 18% to 31%. Pipeline value generated in Month 6 alone exceeded the total pipeline generated across all of Q1 at engagement start. At Month 18, the account is scaling toward a KES 80K/month target spend with maintained SQL economics.

Premium Kenyan apparel brand, direct-to-consumer, average order value KES 2,000. Entered engagement running 14 simultaneous Meta campaigns with overlapping audiences, no creative testing methodology, and a ROAS of 1.8x against a breakeven threshold of 2.6x.

Technical Challenge

The Meta account contained 14 active campaigns with no coherent segmentation — prospecting and retargeting audiences overlapped across 9 of 14 campaigns, meaning the platform competed with itself in the auction and artificially inflated CPMs. The pixel was browser-based only, with no server-side CAPI, resulting in estimated 28–34% event loss post-iOS 14.5. Purchase events fired inconsistently: audit revealed duplicate purchase fires on ~19% of transactions, overstating reported ROAS by an estimated 0.6x. The top-spending ad had run for 11 months without a controlled variant. Frequency: 8.4 in the 30-day window. TikTok generated zero attributable conversions against KES 8,000 monthly spend.

14 Overlapping CampaignsNo Server-Side CAPI19% Duplicate Purchase FiresFrequency 8.428–34% Event Loss
Strategy Deployed

Week 1: Deployed Meta CAPI via sGTM with event deduplication logic using a composite event_id. Purchase event parity confirmed at 94% signal match. Duplicate pixel fires eliminated via GTM trigger sequencing.

Weeks 2–4: Collapsed 14 campaigns to 3: Cold Prospecting (Advantage+ creative), Warm Retargeting (1–7 day site visitors segmented by product page depth), and Retention (purchasers 1–90 days, cross-sell sequences). Audience exclusion logic enforced — zero overlap verified.

Weeks 4–8: 3×3 creative test matrix deployed: 3 hook variants × 3 value proposition framings. Each ad ran at KES 500/day with success criteria defined in advance (CTR >2.8%, ROAS >3.0x at 500 impression minimum). TikTok rebuilt with Spark Ads using UGC from top 12% of customers by LTV.

Final Quantifiable Outcome

At Week 19, blended ROAS reached 6.2x against a breakeven of 2.6x. CPA declined 41% from KES 180 to KES 110. Creative testing produced 11 winning ad concepts over 14 months with documented average lifespan of 6.3 weeks. New customer acquisition increased 178% YoY measured against CRM first-purchase records. TikTok contributed 14% of total new customer acquisition in Month 12 — a channel that had previously generated zero measurable return.

Technical Challenge

CMA regulation created compounding constraints: ad copy required compliance sign-off, restricting creative testing velocity; certain keyword categories carried YMYL algorithmic sensitivity. The standard acquisition funnel was misaligned with a 3–9 month consideration cycle during which prospects research extensively before self-identifying. The existing Google Search campaign targeted high-intent terms exclusively at KES 80–150 CPCs, producing 4–6 leads per month at KES 1,500 CPL. Domain Rating was 14, content infrastructure was non-existent, and no strategy existed to intercept the research phase of the buyer journey.

CMA Compliance Constraints3–9 Month Consideration CycleKES 1,500 Cost Per Lead1.4% Landing Page CVRDomain Rating 14
Strategy Deployed

Months 1–3: Landing page rebuilt under compliance framework. Split-tested 3 variants; winner converted at 3.1% (121% improvement). Paid search expanded to mid-funnel research terms with content-asset CTAs, reducing cost-per-engagement from KES 1,500 to KES 600.

Months 3–12: Keyword cluster model built across 6 topic pillars. 42 long-form articles produced under compliance framework, targeting informational terms with DR-adjusted ranking probability above 60%. Digital PR generated 18 placements in Kenyan financial media, producing 24 linking root domains and lifting DR from 14 to 41.

Months 12–24: Organic traffic converting via newsletter to a 12-email nurture sequence, moving subscribers to consultation over an average 4.2-month email engagement period. Blended CPL declined 53% as organic channel scale offset paid dependency.

Final Quantifiable Outcome

By Month 24, organic search delivered 62% of total qualified consultations at an amortised CPL of KES 400 vs. paid channel's KES 800. 47 target keywords ranked Page 1. Total pipeline in Year 2 reached KES 1.2M against a total agency investment of KES 240K — an 11.7x return on marketing investment at pipeline level, before accounting for a 34% average close rate. Organic traffic in Month 24 was 520% above Month 1 baseline with no additional content investment in the final quarter.
